Old Town Bobovac

Bobovac is the most important and best-fortified town in medieval Bosnia, built on a steep, columnar rock on the southern slopes of the mountain massif Dragovski and Mijakovski poljice above the mouth of the Mijakovska river in Bukovica, southwest of Vareš. It is located not far from the villages of Mijakovići and Dragovići, in the municipality of Vareš. The town can be approached from two directions, from Kraljeva Sutjeska or from Vareš.

It consisted of an upper town with a square tower, the remains of which still exist today, and a lower town, on a step about 20 meters lower, polygonal in shape about 40 meters long and about 25 meters wide, from which the yard and the well can be recognized today. The royal burial chapel has been reconstructed. Today there is a mausoleum of the Kotromanić dynasty. In 2001, the Minister of Defense of the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Mijo Anić, organized Bobovac with the army. Since 2001, a pilgrimage to Bobovac has been traditionally held on October 25, the memorial day of Blessed Queen Katarina.
