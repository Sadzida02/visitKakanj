The Viewpoint Tajan

Tajan is a protected nature area and is located in the territories of the municipalities of Kakanj (15.4%) and Zavidovići (84.4%), Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bosnia and Herzegovina. The highest altitude of the natural monument is 1,297 m

Several endemic plants of the Dinaric system grow in the area of the Tajan Nature Monument. The most important endemic plant that grows only in the Tajan mountain area is Gregersen's milkweed (Euphorbia gregersenii fam. Euphorbiaceae). The species is protected and is on the international red list of threatened plant species (1997 IUCN Red list of threatened plants).

The area of the Tajan Nature Monument abounds in wild animals because the entire area is uninhabited and covered by forests. In addition to large mammals, it is necessary to mention the cave species of insects, spiders and reptiles that inhabit numerous underground cavities. The caves stand out as an ideal winter habitat for bats, which are highly endangered in Europe (the most endangered mammal) and brown bears. Brook trout and crayfish abound in the streams. Paleontological findings up to 15,000 years old, species that became extinct in the Pleistocene, cave bear (ursus spelaeus), cave hyena (Cave hyaena) and red deer (Cervus elephus) were discovered in these caves. An endemic species was found in Ukrasna Pećina, which was named after the Tajan cave weasel (Cyphonetes tajanus).
