Ponijeri Mountain

Ponijeri is a mountain resort at an altitude of 1 200 meters, which is about 20 kilometers from the center of Kakanj. Due to its altitude, it is also an aerial spa. The name Ponijeri comes from the word ponor or plunging, because you can find sinkholes in several places. After the Second World War, tests were carried out on Ponijeri by dyeing the water of the streams there. The water that sinks in the center of Ponijer has been proven to appear somewhere at the bottom of Ponijer in the form of a spring.

The Ponijeri excursion site is part of the Ravna Planina massif, which stretches between the municipalities of Kakanj, Zavidovići, Vareš and Zenica, with the highest peak, Tajan, at an altitude of 1,297 meters. At the foot of the Ponijer, a large number of rivers and streams spring up, the most famous of which are: Suha, Marošićka river, Žuća, Ribnica, Mala Rijeka, Boriva and Lužnica. It is an integral part of the Tajan Nature Monument. Geologically, the Ponijer area is very interesting.

The central part of Ponijer is built of Triassic limestones, which stretches from the source of Žuća in the west to Omlatko and the Trstionica Rainforest in the east. With the presence of water and tectonic activity, the limestones created karst formations such as a small karst field in the center of Ponijer, 3 larger karst bays, numerous sinkholes, caves, pits... The waters that sink from the area of Ponijersko polje and Opaka Ravan (6 of them) appear as the source of the river Žuća, protected by law as a strict natural reserve, while the sinkholes from the area of Močila and Omlatka spring as Djedova vrela in the basin of the Boriva and Trstionica rivers. The northern, western, and eastern parts of Ponijer are part of the Bosnian Seprnetin zone, which is formed by basalts, seprentinites, and peridotites in this area , shale, diabase and partly marl. Here you can find minerals such as quartz, jasper, limonite, seprentine, olivine, opal, hematite, calcite, gypsum, lyxune...
